The ill fated lives
of the writers and the interracial cast who created Gone With the Wind.
are beyond coincidence.

Clark Gable, waiting at the foot of the mountain
where the plane carrying his wife Carole Lombard had crashed.
She was on her way home from selling a record number of war bonds.

Actor, filmmaker Leslie Howard who was making films for the war effort.
He died when the plane he was taking home to England,
was shot down by German fighter planes,
although British Intelligence knew in advance of the attack.

Hattie McDaniel, the first African American to win an Academy Award,
was only 57 years old when she died.
Her father, a Baptist minister, was born a slave and fought with the Union Army.

Sidney Howard, who wrote the screenplay for Gone With the Wind,
died when he was run over by a tractor.

At the intersection of Peachtree and 13th in Atlanta,
Margaret Mitchell was run down in 1949.
