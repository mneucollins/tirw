In 1961, writer Ernest Hemingway went to the Mayo clinic under an assumed name
because he was depressed and suicidal.
Although FBI records now confirm that Hemingway was under surveillance,
he was having difficulty convincing his friends and family
that the FBI was stalking him.
In a chilling incident of collaborative FBI and medical profession gang stalking,
a psychiatrist at the clinic contacted the FBI
and then told Hemingway that the FBI knew he had registered under an assumed name.
A few months later Ernest Hemingway committed suicide.
