And suddenly I was looking down on Cold Spring Harbor.
At first I thought it was Woods Hole where I once spent a summer
(another place where the coastline of the deep blue Atlantic Ocean
is ringed by bioscience laboratories)
but (from an image I had seen on the Internet)
I recognized the pink-arched central facade
of the Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory Library,
the Grace Auditorium, the endless laboratories
housed in clapboard houses and old mansions.
It was late afternoon and, as if haunted by the dark eugenics past
of the Eugenics Record Office at Cold Spring Harbor,
shadows surrounded the buildings.
