The dream began in a laboratory.
A woman was bending over a faded gray photograph,
the equipment surrounding her from another era of laboratory time.

As if I had I clicked on a zoom-in button
(like those arrows on ubiquitous Internet-situated maps
of places where I longed to go)
a clear X-shaped pattern emerged in the center of the photograph.
Instantly I recognized Rosalind Franklin's famous X-Ray diffraction photograph
that with such startling elegance described the helical shape of DNA.

She turned and tried to speak to me
but the dream dissolved into another dream.
