I was at home watching the 1971 World Series with my Dad
when Roberto Clemente hit the home run
that started the Pittsburgh Pirates on their victory over the Baltimore Orioles.
Clemente, who was born in Puerto Rico, was voted the series MVP.
I remember the celebration in Pittsburgh after the game.

A little more than a year later, I had moved to California,
but I went home for holidays.
I was home with my family when Roberto Clemente died on New Year's Eve.
He was personally taking food and supplies to earthquake-stricken Nicaragua.
when the plane he was on crashed near the coast of Puerto Rico.

I remember the pleasure of watching Clemente
in the field and at the plate

and the candlelight vigil on the Isla Verde beach.
