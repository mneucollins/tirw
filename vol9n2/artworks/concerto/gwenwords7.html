In a Shanghai prison during the Cultural Revolution
on the paper supplied by his jailors for "self criticism",
Chinese artist Mu Xin silently creates an extraordinary work of calligraphy.

In a Berkeley apartment, an American artist tethered to an imposed profession
creates a work of art on index cards.

Later, when he was put under house arrest,
Mu Xin created a series of ethereal landscape memories.

Each painted on a small piece of paper,
each invoking the memory of the countryside that nurtured his spirit,
and was now denied.
