Japanese American Chiura Obata created transcendent paintings
of the California hills,
the mountains, the waterfalls,
bridges over streams, rocks and water.

And he taught art at the University of California in Berkeley.

But along with more than 100,000 other Japanese Americans,
he was imprisoned in 1942 by the US Government.

Then at Tanforan and Topaz, he taught art to his fellow prisoners, and he drew
the camps in which he was confined.
I remember a watercolor of a woman and her children. Behind barbed wire,
they are reading posted regulations.
Unreachable in the far distance
are the mountains and hills that he painted when he was free.

After the War, Chiura Obata was reinstated as a professor of art at Cal.
I imagine him hiking with the Sierra Club,
once again sketching and painting
the mountains of California.
