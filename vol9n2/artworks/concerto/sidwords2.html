The other sculpture I have been looking at is "Unconquered II",
Allan Houser's memorial for the Apache people.
Beside a photograph of this sculpture
are the words that begin Sioux Chief Yellow Hawk's morning prayer:

"Great Spirit
Whose voice I hear in the winds
Hear me"

Allan Houser's father was with the Warm Springs Chiricahuas
when Geronimo surrendered to the U.S. Army.
When 1200 Chiricahuas were put on cattle cars and sent to prisons in Florida,
his father was with them.
In a prison camp in Alabama, where the surviving members of the tribe were shunted,
his mother was born in captivity
When the last of the Chircahuas were sent to Fort Sill,
his parents were with them.
And there they remained captive for twenty-three years.

In 1914 when they were finally freed,
Allan Houser was the first child born out of captivity.
