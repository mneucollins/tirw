The losses to our culture are relentless.
I set them forth not to imply that tragedy is the fate of every artist,
but rather because I think that if they are documented,
it will become clear that there are patterns,
that the losses are beyond coincidence.

About 25 years ago, I watched Bill Jones and Arnie Zane dance together.
I was in New York City. Alone. Looking at art.
And I went to see Blauvelt Mountain not knowing what to expect.

I have never forgotten that performance.
The relationship between the two men --
Arnie, Jewish from the Bronx,
Bill, African American from upstate New York --
the shared path of dance that they created together.

A few year later, Arnie Zane was dead. Lost to AIDS.
