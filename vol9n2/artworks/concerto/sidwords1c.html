Remember Ernest Hemingway

Depressed and suicidal, in 1961,
he went to the Mayo clinic under an assumed name.

Although FBI records now confirm that Hemingway was under surveillance,
he was having difficulty convincing his friends and family
that the Federal Bureau of Investigation was watching his every move.

In a chilling incident
of collaborative intelligence agency and medical profession stalking of artists,
a psychiatrist at the clinic contacted the FBI
and then told Hemingway
that the FBI knew he had registered under an assumed name.
The Mayo Clinic gave Hemingway shock treatments;
he lost his memory and ability to write.

A few months later, Ernest Hemingway committed suicide.
