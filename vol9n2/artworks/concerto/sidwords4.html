I am very happy to be a curator in these times
when bringing the spirit of art and the people who create it to a wider public
is particularly important. For me, that is my life's work.

And yet I am troubled by what happened to Robert Blackburn.
I speak out of turn.
He himself was devoted to his work at the Printmaking Workshop he founded.
He was enormously influential in introducing the techniques of printmaking
to so many other artists.

Yet.... And I am thinking about this because I have been looking at such patterns --
how the careers of artists are diverted --
I would have wished for him a life where his own work
was as equally celebrated as the workshop
that in the spirit of helping other artists,
he founded and nurtured throughout his life.
