 A more sacred word with her than most, other words or other people. She has a firm sense of the term and yet resists any definition (it is a personal trait, it made her a good mother: her children never in a category). A good lover also. I know you think we are in a rut but we are not, she says of their lovemaking. You will see.

All over the house things of beauty and differentiation, likewise resisting categories. A French Quarter tourist dish from the 30's next to a woven lunch basket from Tibet. Had we more order or better furniture, he says, we could be in a magazine. We could do the same thing if we took our clothes off, she laughs.
