We sleep in the high bed in the cabin. In the morning the sun comes through the windows and lights like a sparrow on the blond pine floor, then on the white cotton blanket, and finally on my face, rousing me. Despite the sun, the air in the cabin is so pure and cold my nose tingles. I wriggle deeper under the covers, wrap myself around the curve of his spine. He is warm with sleep. The sheets smell of lavender.
