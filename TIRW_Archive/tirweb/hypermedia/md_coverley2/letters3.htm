Clara to me,

July 30, 1986

Dear Laura:

I am so disappointed and frustrated, I guess I don't care if the censorial snoops see this. When last I wrote you, I was anticipating that Mikhail and I would be married in June of this year.

Suddenly, though, in the way these things happen in Russia, our permission just disappeared. I would say there was no reason, but I think there was a reason. Mikhail was reassigned to a different agency--a promotion of sorts. But our Delegation is not on the approved list, so we couldn't be married because I couldn't go north with him.

He has been gone since June 14, in S., and I have heard nothing. The good news is that he gets to keep the apartment here. So, I am spending my free time this summer stitching up curtains, dish cloths, and table settings so that he will feel at home when he returns in September. I, however, continue to live officially at Alyona's. (photo is of Alyona's daughter and her friend). Perhaps the political situation will improve by next year, and we can enter a proper married life.

Love, Clara
