Clara to me, received after her death.

June 21, 1998

Dear Laura--

After what seems like an endless journey, some by bus, much of it on foot, I have arrived at last in Arkhangelsk. I went immediately to the apartment bloc that was the last address I had from Mikhail.  It was nearly abandoned, except for some few squatters, living there without heat or water.  They knew nothing of the former residents except that the goverment had moved them to another city, maybe Pevek, deep in the interior. 

It seems so long ago that Mikhail and I spent that idyllic three weeks on an island near here, certain that Russia had a future, and we had a future, and that it would be together.  Now, the country, Mikhail's country, falls apart, and we, too, are shattered.  I don't believe that this is the end of the earth, but you can see it from here.

 

Love, Clara
