Clara to me,


May 1, 1981

Dear Laura:

I am sending this letter to you through Mr. Royce. Hope he remembers to drop it off with you. I would appreciate it if you could also mail the letter and duplicate photos for my Mom.

You were the first to warn me against taking this job--so far from home and such a strange place! And, as usual, your insight, foresight, were infallible. Still, I have endured. I have a nice room in a private house here--a luxury in itself. My landlady, Alyona, and her daughter, Evgeny, are most considerate. (The photo is of Alyona taking me shopping on the Nevsky Prospekt). Just now, the winter is about to loosen its hold on us for a few weeks. Soon everyone will joyfully throw coats and cares aside and stroll along the avenues, lean out over the sapphire blue canals, laugh in the white light of the midnight sun. This is a beautiful city.

Still, the dark, first few months would have been grim without the companionship of one of the officials here--a scientist with the government--Mikhail Pyotr Berglund. He's of mixed descent (half Swedish). His research is mostly in the arctic and he spends summers searching the northernmost seas. We have planned a celebration for the magic night--right before he leaves.

I am not sure how much our little committee will accomplish here, but I can tell you that the natives believe the atmosphere is changing. A global warming of the hands at the campfire of freedom. There is much suppressed optimism.
Mr. Royce will return here in July, so you can send letters with him.

Your friend, Clara
