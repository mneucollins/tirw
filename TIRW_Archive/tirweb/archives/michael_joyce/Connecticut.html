In the seam of hills they moved unwittingly into Connecticut and back out again much as a steam engine in a travelogue pulls a string of bright cars through sleepy Alpine valleys. Here briefly they came upon a slope of thin snow on an unseasonable afternoon, children in parkas daring traffic in a hurry to ride this skim, gaily colored composite snowboards over their shoulders. The snow smear was, of course, artificial. Not long after they stopped for coffee and tarts at a French bakery, again back in rural New York.
