LISTENING AT THE PICTURES
A new sound work for the cinema

Your chance to hear The Sixth Shining Wizard Sense, Bill and Ted's Alien Apocalypse, A Clockwork Bladerunner and 2001: An Aladdin Odyssey.

Played on rotation between film screenings at the cinema was a new work of sound art created by Scanner during an artists residency at Reading College, England.

Working in collaboration with students from the Music Technology Department, Scanner created new stories and films of fancy using a collage of fragments from film dialogue mixed with imaginary soundtracks.

Scanner says of "Listening at the Pictures": "I wished to develop something innovative off-site, a work that was very public and yet could also include contributions from the students. I began thinking about a place that brought different groups of people together, somewhere intimate and quiet, that could enhance any experience of listening and chanced upon the idea of using a local cinema as a possible venue. The venue itself was the catalyst for the final work 'conscious that we hear films as much as we see them.'"

"Listening at the Pictures" was commissioned by Art at the Centre, Reading in partnership with Reading College and was originally presented in collaboration with Warner Village Cinemas.
